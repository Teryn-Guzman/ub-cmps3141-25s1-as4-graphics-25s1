Exercise 4: Evaluating Learnability of a Vector Graphics Editor

Tool Used

For this exercise, I evaluated Figma. When I first opened Figma, the interface appeared clean and modern, but also overwhelming. While the layout suggested professional capabilities, it lacked clear onboarding guidance for first-time users.

Challenges

Several aspects of Figma were not immediately learnable and required outside help. One major challenge was understanding the differences between frames, groups, and components, as their purposes were not immediately apparent. I also struggled with basic tasks such as resizing objects proportionally, aligning elements precisely, and understanding how layers interacted with each other.

Another feature that was not obvious was the ability to convert text to vector outlines. I had to search online to learn that this action is called “Outline stroke” in Figma and that it permanently converts text into shapes. Exporting files as SVG rather than raster formats also required additional research.

Time on Task

It took approximately 150 minutes to complete the business card, letterhead, and envelope designs. A considerable portion of this time was spent learning how to use the interface, locating tools, and correcting mistakes rather than designing.

Assessment

While Figma is a powerful and flexible tool, it could be more learnable for beginners. The application would benefit from clearer visual signals, contextual tooltips, and a guided tutorial that walks new users through common tasks such as creating frames, aligning elements, and exporting vector files.

Comparison to Other Tools

Prior to this assignment, I had experience using simpler design tools such as Canva. Compared to Canva, Figma offers significantly more control and precision but is less forgiving for beginners. Tasks that are automated or simplified in Canva required manual setup and technical understanding in Figma.
